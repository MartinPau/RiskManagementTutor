Orderly People Style Guide (style.md)

This style guide captures the visual essence of Orderly People, designed to ensure consistency across all digital touchpoints.

Brand Personality

Orderly People projects a professional, trustworthy, and human-centric image. The design is clean, structured (as the name implies), and relies on high-quality photography and intentional whitespace.

Color Palette

The brand uses a sophisticated, low-saturation palette that feels both corporate and modern.





Primary: #1a365d (Deep Navy Blue - for headers, primary actions, and brand presence)



Background: #f7fafc (Off-white/Soft Grey - for main surface areas)



Surface Dim: #d7dadc (For subtle differentiation in sections)



Container High: #ffffff (Pure White - for cards and focus areas)



Text: #1a202c (Dark Charcoal - for high legibility)

Typography





Primary Font: Merriweather (Serif)



Usage: Used for headings to convey authority, tradition, and trustworthiness.



Secondary/Body Font: Sans-serif (Inter or similar)



Usage: Used for body text and UI elements for modern clarity and readability.

Visual Language





Roundness: ROUND_FOUR (Subtle 4px rounding on buttons and containers) for a balanced, approachable feel.



Imagery: High-fidelity, professional portraits with natural lighting. Authentic human connection is a key theme.



Logo: The wordmark "Orderly People" in a clean sans-serif font, usually in white or primary navy depending on background.

Component Patterns





Navigation: Clean, top-aligned navigation with clear hierarchy.



Cards: Lightly elevated or outlined cards with generous internal padding.



Buttons: Solid primary color blocks with minimal decoration.
